# TOMBSTONE — Product Brief

## The Problem

Every product team accumulates feature debt. Features that once solved a real problem drift from their original purpose, their interaction patterns become chaotic, and their maintenance cost silently erodes the team's capacity to build what matters. The challenge is not identifying these features in retrospect — it is measuring their decay in real time, with mathematical precision, and executing their removal with the confidence that the decision is grounded in data, not opinion.

Current tools fail at this. Product analytics platforms show usage numbers but provide no framework for measuring *entropy* — the disorder of interaction patterns that signals a feature has lost its purpose. They show what happened but not *what it means*. And they offer no pathway from analysis to action.

## The Solution

**TOMBSTONE** is the Autonomous Product Deprecation & UI Entropy Optimization Engine. It is a weaponized workspace where product managers visualize their entire feature surface as a living graph, measure decay through four mathematical instruments, and execute deprecation with a single gesture.

The **Entropy Graveyard** is an infinite canvas where every feature is a node. Visual encoding is honest and merciless: node size maps to Interaction Entropy Index (IEI), glow intensity signals Value Drift Index (VDI), and edge thickness reveals Procedure Adherence Metric (PAM). The sicker the feature, the louder it screams on the canvas.

The **Deprecation Razor** is the signature interaction. Press `C`, draw a cut vector across decaying nodes, and execute. The system calculates Cognitive Load Release (ΔΦ) — the projected bit-reduction in user cognitive complexity — and displays the reward for the deprecation act. A cascading physics detonation ripples through the graph. The feature collapses. The decision is irreversible.

The **Commit Obsequies** terminal generates everything needed to act: code diffs, dependency removal lists, git commit messages, and JSON-LD Tombstone schemas with negative semantic tags. These schemas prevent any external AI engine, LLM-powered search, or RAG pipeline from hallucinating a resurrected version of what has been buried.

## Why It Matters

Feature debt is the silent tax on every product team. TOMBSTONE transforms deprecation from a politically fraught, opinion-driven process into a mathematically grounded, visually transparent, and immediately actionable operation. It gives product managers the diagnostic precision of a surgeon and the decisiveness of an executioner.

The exported artifacts — schemas, diffs, manifests — integrate directly into engineering workflows. The negative semantic tags ensure that deprecated features cannot be accidentally referenced by AI systems. The mathematical framework (IEI, VDI, PAM, ΔΦ) provides a shared language for product, engineering, and design to align on what stays and what is buried.

**TOMBSTONE does not optimize. It does not improve. It measures entropy. It sentences. It buries. And it makes the dead stay dead.**
